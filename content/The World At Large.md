# Eight thousand years after the end of humanity as we know it, life speeds ahead ever faster.

  

## The Solar System

It is ever a human thing to begin in a most unfamiliar time and place with what we regard as the most familiar and not the most understandable. In honoring that human nature, let us mark an unfamiliar beginning by beginning on Dirt: Third (formerly fourth and even more formerly third again) of the planets that circle the birth star of humanity and closer to a nature reserve than a populated world. Through moon-fall and mass-extinction, life has faced its precipitous end time and time again and dragged itself from the precipice of annihilation. The scoured South sprawls into vast grasslands where there was scorched desert, and the tropics once again teem with budding life. Though quaint settlements sit quietly on the coasts of this paler, bluer dot, make no mistake. This world is as home to the space-faring folk of the Solar System as a baby’s crib is a bed to an adult. Dirt is the place for remembrances and nostalgia—a site for revanchists and revisionists to blindly worship in reverence and the seat of learning of the abstract arts. The stage on which the tale of humanity unfolds is not here, nor may it ever be ever again.

Mercury fortified, Venus abandoned, and Mars ancient, humanity of its birth star stretches itself far beyond the confines of its first planet. The city skylines of the Jovian moons, the living fleet of Saturn, and the great cantons of Uranus and Neptune show life beyond planetary gravity. A tourist steps onto the massive Solar Gun station orbiting high out of the plane of a Saturnine ring and begins their sabbatical in the Martian summer on the Tharsis plateau in two hours; a roving knight chases a serial arsonist in a darting jaunt from Umbriel to Triton and catches the perpetrator stealing combustibles on a gas mine floating in the super-dense critical phase deep below Jupiter’s atmosphere. The world gets bigger as interface and grid technology brings humanity’s longest stride far beyond the distance between planets. The world shrinks ever smaller as the web of communication grows at a pace even greater than the cross-planet strides of humanity’s transportation.

Everyone is more of a tourist. Everyone has less of a home.

At the frontiers of the Kuiper Belt, on the neon-drowned port world of Pluto, the waves of nonhuman and intragalactic influence crash onto the human world like waves upon an eroding shore. People theorize and question the isolation of humanity from the vast world of life intelligent or otherwise beyond their own star system: Six millennia of missing history, past grudges completely forgotten, one-sided blood feuds across entire species, and yet it seems not one human can recall the reason. What appeared to most like self-imposed isolationism becomes ever more clearly part embargo and part quarantine; the nonhumans are not being kept out; the humans are being kept in.

## Andromeda Approaching

Arriving hundreds of million years early, the entire galaxy of intelligent life turns their instruments to gaze upon the merger of two galaxies in real time. As billions of stars begin their great coordinated dance across hundreds of millennia and thousands of light-years, some point their telescopes toward these visiting stars and discovering something utterly bizarre.

Strange life, born not of evolution and chance, but tragedy and yearning cross the interstellar void and capriciously flutters in the darkness of the night sky, accepting the unknowing invitation of innocuous observers. These odd folk’s pale marble forms, etched with unknown language and formed into incongruous shapes, herald their species’ long awaited renewal. With trepidation and curiosity, a few individuals from two entirely different realities of life collide and intertwine like their galactic provenances, bringing into existence new relationships never considered possible. However, with all great shifts in paradigm come winners and losers; perpetrators and victims. Will this serendipitous and monumental meeting of worlds bring new harmony, or will the tangle of these intertwining lives spin out into even greater tragedy?

## The Great Return

The civilization built around humanity’s birth star has been revealed to be but one of countless kaleidoscopic expressions of human potential. In the wake of the fall of ancient hegemonies, humanity has spread itself far and wide across the galaxy, establishing new homes and even integrating with the endless diversity of life where humans find it. However, history’s reach far outpaces its memory, and the past of humanity’s deeds have brought these disparate civilizations back to the site of their collective exodus all those millennia ago.

Traveling among the stars and wandering on great starlight-capturing vessels come the people of the Garden Worlds. Eschewing the logic of accumulation and rejecting the inevitability of power’s concentration, the vastly varying cultures and creeds of the Garden Worlds share first and foremost their rejection of traditional hierarchy and propriety. Though through their wandering world’s travels their denizens come and go, a gardener can always be identified by their unusual resourcefulness and affinity for having too many names for the same thing—sometimes for the same person. Possessing a deep connection to the old history of humanity, the gardeners’ technology built on complex vows within codified rituals exists as a parallel evolution of the Solar System’s thinking machines and programmed logics. The Garden Worlds are mystified as the birthplace of great warriors, especially by other humans, a reputation bolstered by many gardeners’ willingness to challenge existing power structures wherever they go.

Even more connected to the atrocity-filled past is the Principate, whose megalithic vessels float in space like colossal vigils to what was lost and what must not happen again. Possessing the secrets of the construction and management of interstellar highways, the Principate act as the infrastructural managers of the world at large, ferrying massive fleets across thousands of light years in mere seconds. Unlike the free spirits of their once-allies, the Principate watch and purvey, allowing the civilizations of the galaxy to appoint the Principate’s body of Executor agents to carry out the functions of the highways. Though the massive tombstone-like crafts of the Principate have all but stripped themselves of all organic context, its secrets and its mission are inseparable from its past as an organization key to the fall of the old order. Though most consider them a shadowy middleman, the Principate are still known to further their own agenda through the shadow of suggestion and information, subtly setting the agenda of the most powerful organizations in the world at large.

## A Collapsing House

In this vertex world, where the Gods’ gaze so rarely falls, an attempt to reach the now-unreachable pinnacles of divinity was made eight thousand years ago, and the failure of that attempt shifted the nature of the vertex world’s reality forever. Millennia after the initial failed attempt, the demigods of all things across all worlds languish in their failure, shattered and broken in their almighty incapacity. However, the time has come for the barrier to divinity to fall. The suffering of the many has been heard on high, and salvation is soon at hand—

So why do so many fight with every ounce of their being to stop it?
