#progression 

One of the keystones of practitioner progression, phantasm proficiency governs the core competencies surrounding use of the [[[Phantasm|phantasm]] in all its aspects. Its core competencies are tiered such that progression to the next level generally implies competence of the current level. As with other progression keystones, specializations also begin after a certain level of proficiency, leading to deeper and more divergent use cases and stranger applications.

## Description

#### **Phantasm Proficiency I**

> Can learn *Amplify*. Reach can be extended 10 times further for each tier of highest affinity. 

At the most basic level of phantasm proficiency, a practitioner is merely capable of extending the [[Domain|domain]] of their influence beyond their physical body. At the beginning, a practitioner may only be able to conceptualize the edges of their phantasm's domain as "contact", but it is possible through experience and practice alone to gain access to the entire volume of the phantasm's domain even at this stage. If the practitioner has awakened a phase disposition, they will gain access to phase distortion as well.

Phantasms are entirely invisible at this stage to the practitioner, and grasping the presence of a phantasm is difficult. However, when a practitioner acts via their phantasm, a faint distortion akin to a heat wave will be visible to all individuals with Phantasm Proficiency I and baseline PRC. Effort is required at this point to conceal the action of a phantasm, and most [[Technique#Apophatic Techniques|apophatic techniques]] are unavailable. The only exception to this rule is learning [[Living Force]].

==**Guideline:** Check PRC often at this stage passively and challenge PRC often when attempting to perform precise maneuvers of any kind. If Living Force is not learned, the first attempt will challenge AGI.==
## Evolution

#### **Phantasm Proficiency II**

> Can learn *Reinforce, Pierce*, and *Imbue.* Gain access to, [[Barrier Technique|barrier techniques]], [[Domain Technique|domain techniques]], and all [[Creation Technique|cataphatic techniques]] except [[Technique Extension|technique extension]] and [[Technique Distillation|technique distillation]].

Many apophatic and cataphatic techniques open up to an practitioner who has grasped the fundamental components of the phantasm:

- A volume known as a domain, with a fixed total strength. Local differences in strength produce stronger actions that reflect in the techniques' added effectiveness and constraints.
- A potentially existent boundary that can be expressed as a barrier with a limited durability and  [[Barrier Technique#Barrier Definition|boundary conditions]] that the practitioner can use to partition space.
- Separate instances of the phantasm, known as [[Instance|instances]], that have limited control and priority but persist for some time.
- A pressure-like measure of intensity known as a [[Gradient|gradient]] that determines the local strength of the domain.

Within this framework, the Schools of Sidh developed the foundations of practitioner field and combat techniques:

- The School of Ionia developed movement and detection techniques utilizing the basic use of domains.
- The School of Phoenicia refined instances and produced the fundamental catalogue of instance shapes that are now used ubiquitously throughout the world. Additionally, they added onto the existing movement and detection techniques that the Ionia school invented.
- The School of Sidh drastically accelerated understanding and construction of barriers, creating techniques that significantly strength, modify, or weaken barriers. They also provided new domain techniques while putting forth the first gradient techniques.

Phantasms become even harder to detect for those without proficiency, but become plainly perceptible without effort to practitioners. Certain techniques can still conceal a phantasm, but passive PRC checks can reveal them. At this stage, most basic actions like expanding/retracting the phantasm and focusing the domain become natural and do not require checks at all to succeed. 

==**Guideline:** Novel applications of these baseline techniques such as to Color invocation generally require an associated difficulty 10 check on the first successful attempt. When in doubt, check INT.==

#### **Phantasm Proficiency III**

> Can learn all phantasm-based types. Gain access to technique inversion, structural techniques, technique extension and technique distillation. 

At this level practitioners have grasped all fundamental aspects of phantasm control, including the interactions between each component of the phantasm. These include:

- Effects of rotational gradients on effectiveness and boundary conditions in barriers.
- Boundary condition interactions caused by multiple barriers within the same domain.
- Strengths and weaknesses of different apophatic techniques in terms of integrating cataphatic techniques.
