#ontokinetics 

In ontokinetics, the term Phantasm refers to the space occupied by a perceiving actor in reality. This space defines the neighborhood of communicable signs that are directly interfacing with the perceiving actor.  Different forms of perception generate different characteristic phantasms that are fundamentally different, and differing sets of internal ontologies impact the specifics of a phantasm's characteristics. Phantasms stand at the center of all [[Technique|technique]], and the study of how phantasms can impact communicable signs is known as Ontokinetics.

The phantasm is fundamentally comprised of two fundamental components:

- A volume occupying the space equipped with real measures of distance, known as a **[[Domain (Ontokinetics)|Domain]]**.
- A boundary that may or may not possess certain continuity conditions. If continuity conditions exist and are well-defined, they are known as **[[Barrier Technique|Barriers]]**.

In addition, phantasms can also possess at any time a collection of disconnected components that have domains and/or barriers known as **[[Instance|Instances]].**
