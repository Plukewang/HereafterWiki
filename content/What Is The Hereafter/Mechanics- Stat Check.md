# How Do Stat Checks Work?

Each stat you have comes with a base number, a roll, and bonuses that are calculated on the stack. When a stat is checked, the outcome is typically equal to

$Outcome = base + bonus + roll$

The base is calculated at the specific instance of the stack along with the bonus, while the roll is determined by the maximum roll of the stat. If the outcome of a stat check is greater than or equal to the requisite for success, the check is successful, otherwise, the check fails. If the outcome is 5 or more greater than the requisite, the check is a critical success.

## Check Results

Degrees of success and failure are typically determined by the GM. However, the specificity of the success or failure depends on the specificity of the check itself and the roll. For lower rolled checks, outcomes should typically be judged entirely on the performance of the base and bonus, while higher-rolled checks should be considered “lucky” in narrative. That is to say that there are no “unlucky” outcomes, except in rare circumstances where negative rolls are present.

  
Critical successes are determined at the discretion of the GM, but should at least fulfill the direct outcome of the attempted stat check.

If a stat check fails within 5 of the requisite outcome, and another relevant stat is greater than or equal to the requisite, the other stat may chime in and provide a hint or an additional bonus.

## How Do I Improve Stat Checks?

- Increase the base stat through feats and intrinsic traits.

- Increase the maximum roll through intrinsic or special traits.

- Gain a bonus to the current roll through an effect or extrinsic trait.

- Multiply any portion of the stat check through an effect or a trait.

- Effects that grant “unlimited effectiveness” force a successful stat check.

- Gaining new information or understanding can confer additional bonuses to a check on repeat attempt.

- During Critical Moments, all rolls are maximized, and roll again until the roll is not maximum.
