Projection techniques are a category of cataphatic techniques that translate concepts extracted from Color and project image of the concept onto real space. Projection differs from [[Manifestation Technique]] in both target and final product, as concepts that can be directly manifested often categorically cannot manifest fully in real space.

## Basic Principles

### Augmented Reality

The source of the projection technique is almost always a level [[|ascended]] from concrete real space. Practitioners that utilize projection technique in combat typically choose ideas as their source, while non-violent practitioners most commonly draw from embedded frameworks via [[|algebraic techniques]]. The result is that the effect of projection techniques overlay themselves onto the base level of real space.

The overlay property of projection technique makes the product particularly resilient to physical changes in the target, as they superimpose themselves onto and are thus partially independent of concrete reality.

**A projection technique directly assigns signs to other signs.**

### Projection Metastability

Projection techniques create metastable products that become unstable on interaction with their intended targets. In practice, this metastability means exertion from an opponent or unwanted force of will can cause the projection to become unstable, potentially hampering the desired outcome of the technique. However, this metastability also allows for innate flexibility of outcome for an experienced practitioner, as one can create projections that transform into secondary products when met with enough resistance.

## Applications

Projection techniques divide most neatly into two distinct applications: Compact and Principal Projection.

### Compact Projection

Compact projection renders the holographic image of the concept to be projected in real space in its entirety, allowing compact projections to bring forth a fully interactive form of the concept into reality. This form of projection is reserved for concepts that are meant to stay directly on the field as independent objects, rather than as larger dynamical additions to the space itself.

  

Common forms of compact projection used by practitioners:

- Sequence projection is a commonly used Blue technique in informational systems used to preserve the full informational state of any system if backups or testing is required.

- Identity projection is a technique rare among most practitioners but common among those affine with Red that is typically used to temporarily create copies of an object as substitution in another technique.

### Principal Projection

Principal projection renders the principal components of a larger and more complex concept and creates an image that represents the key components of the concept’s web of interactions. The complexity of principal projection targets allows for the technique to bring into existence much more complex and dynamic concepts that can affect the environment at large, essentially using the innate structure of the concept itself to perform complex calculations without any effort on the part of the practitioner.

  

Common forms of principal projection used by practitioners:

- Spectral projection converts the continuum of information on a target into a simple graph that can easily be manipulated, allowing systems engineers to quickly adjust specific elements on the fly without triggering unwanted effects.

### Mechanical Considerations

Projection techniques are ideal when attempting to subject targets to a specific condition, and not so ideal when the goal is to bring a specific target into existence. As such, focus on using projection techniques when the goal is the achievement of a condition or the imposition of a condition.

Common targeting criteria:

- Contact

- Area

- Temporal

- Combination of the above

Known Applications:

- Sequence Projection (Compact)
    
    - Priority: 2
    
    - Level: 1  
        Create a [[|sequence]] object that is a Blue copy of the skill's target. The sequence object inherits the attributes and actions of the target on skill resolution.
        
        Sequence object: When a sequence object becomes the target of an action, the user can choose another available target for the object to copy.
        
    

- Perspective Projection (Compact)
    
    - Priority: 3
    
    - Level: 1  
        Create a [[|perspective]] object that is an Indigo copy of the event that occurred to the target.
        
        Perspective object: When a perspective object successfully targets an entity, it experiences the event copied by the object.
        
    

- Identity Projection (Compact)
    
    - Priority: 3
    
    - Level: 1  
        Create a copy of the object with 1 HP as Bulk Material.
    

- Origin Projection (Principal)
    
    - Priority: 3
    
    - Level: 2  
        Create a copy of the target location's metrics space. Targets of the metrics space copy must remain equidistant from the copied metric space and the original.
    

- Spectral Projection (Principal)
    
    - Priority: 2
    
    - Level: 1
        
        Create a [[spectrum]] object that is a graphical representation of the target.
        
        Spectrum object: When the spectrum object becomes the target of an ability, also target the target itself.
        
    

- Potential Projection (Compact)
    
    - Priority: 2
    
    - Level: 1
        
        Create a [[potential]] object that starts as a copy of the target with 1 HP as Bulk Material.
        
        Potential object: Sacrifice the object to gain > on the originator of the potential object.
        
    

- Chaos Projection (Principal)
    
    - Priority: 2
    
    - Level: 1
        
        Create an additional roll counter on the target. Check the counter any time a stat check or numerical calculation is made involving the target.
