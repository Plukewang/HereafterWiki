Transmutation techniques define a keystone role among apophatic techniques, being not only the most common type of apophatic technique, but also the categorically most efficient technique, even more than [[Technique Distillation]]. As their name suggests, transmutation techniques transform their target, causing a change in the production of communicable signs in the target. Many commonplace miracles are the result of transmutation techniques, and many divine feats are as well.

---

## Basic Principles

### Stepwise Transmutation

Transmutation techniques act on their targets through the movement of the target’s ontological structure, and because this movement can only be done one step at a time, transmutations that act in a broad scope can become highly inefficient techniques with low output and high requirements. For example, transmutation of water to ice on the broad scope of material type is highly inefficient, producing large amounts of exhaustion, but the transmutation of water to ice on the scope of temperature and pressure is nearly devoid of all exhaustion.

This vast difference between proper and improper usage means transmutation techniques are at once abused by poorly learned practitioners and exploited by highly skilled practitioners as well.

**A transmutation technique changes the structure of communicable signs in real space.**

### Conservation of Information

The first principle of transmutation techniques naturally necessitates the second: communicable signs produced by a target post-transmutation remain connected to the signs the target produced pre-transmutation. That is to say, a reverse transmutation technique always exists for any effective transmutation technique. This principle does not guarantee that the reversal of the transmutation is necessarily as simple as the forward transmutation, but it guarantees the reversal’s existence. Unfortunately for practitioners, the symmetry between forward and reverse transmutations and their complexities are not readily predictable, and thus require large amounts of trial and error to discover.

## Applications

Transmutation techniques hold celebrity status among the cataphatic techniques, as their results are directly impressive to even non-practitioners. Surprisingly, their usefulness does not diminish with either experience or specificity, as almost every technique framework can make use of or require transmutation techniques to function.

  

**Static Transmutation** transmutes the target directly, targeting a static ontological structure and inducing a reversible but permanent change. This type of transmutation is used primarily on inanimate objects and effects, with occasional uses outside of that scope:

- Conversion of random surrounding material into vital survival requisites.

- Altering the shape of common objects to suit a specific function.

- Adjusting the nature of living beings to shift their behavior.

**Dynamic Transmutation** adjusts the underlying structure of the target, causing its response to the environment to shift in accordance with the transmutation. Dynamic transmutation is more commonly used to construct components of technique frameworks or ritual objects. Some of these uses include:

- Modifying the imbued technique of a ritual object.

- Imposing or enforcing a desired behavior on a target.

---

## Mechanical Considerations

Transmutation techniques are mainstay techniques for the exploitation of environmental factors in combat. They also possess great use outside of combat, allowing for the transmutation of relevant evidence into potential leads. In most cases, static transmutation will suffice.
