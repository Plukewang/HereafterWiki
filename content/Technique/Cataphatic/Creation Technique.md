Creation techniques are a class of [[Technique]] that explicitly create a communicable sign where there was none, directly violating most physical laws of conservation. As such, creation techniques are almost universally highly inefficient, if not the most inefficient among all cataphatic techniques. Despite this inefficiency, the power to bring into existence anything the practitioner is capable of recreating is immense enough to warrant creation techniques’ frequent usage.

---

## Basic Principles

### Ontological Displacement

When creation techniques create communicable signs, they do not necessarily create it from thin air. Instead, the inception of the technique’s product displaces the ontological structure of its site of creation, encoding the “shadow” of the created sign in its wake. This displacement can be used to recover the original state of the world before the creation, both to the practitioner’s benefit and detriment.

**A creation technique creates the desired sign by redefining and replacing the underlying real signs.**

Additionally, the displacement effect typically causes a rippling “recoil” effect in the space it occupies. In real space, this recoil manifests as a direct force pushing objects away, but the recoil can take other shapes when the creation technique is not used in real space.

### Minimum Fulfillment

Creation techniques always fulfill only the specific criteria of the practitioner’s requirements upon the use of the technique. For example, a practitioner using a creation technique to create a commonplace object from their own conception of the object will produce an object with properties sufficient to match the practitioner’s conception, potentially lacking intrinsic or extrinsic functionalities or properties of the actual object. For this reason, specificity is paramount for all creation techniques, as any vagueness in the conditions will result in even further inefficiency or potential catastrophic failure.

---

## Applications

Creation techniques have obvious value due to their ability to overcome physical scarcity, but inefficiency and specificity lend poorly to everyday usage. As a result, the primary users of creation techniques are advanced users that are specifically bringing a well-designed concept into existence. However, duplication via other techniques are simply more efficient than direct creation, relegating even advanced uses of creation to prototyping or the creation of unique objects.

  

One alternative does however exist to these limited applications, thought it has little to do with the creation aspect. The ontological displacement of creation techniques provides a surprising value to practitioners, as this ontological displacement is extremely powerful when used with discretion. For example, the direct creation of an object in the proximity of another communicable sign displaces that sign due to the recoil of creation. A skilled practitioner can utilize this recoil as a type of displacing denial of a sign, essentially acting as a pseudo-barrier.

## Mechanical Considerations

Creation is really, really bad of a technique for most cases! If there is any other way to create the object from the environment instead of out of thin air, do it!

  

That being said, there is one incredibly powerful use of a creation technique: using it to stuff other techniques. Due to the fact that a creation technique displaces everything around the created object when the technique finishes, a well-timed creation can be used as a form of immediate area-of-denial that cancels active technique activations.
