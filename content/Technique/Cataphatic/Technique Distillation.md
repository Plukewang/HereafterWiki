Technique distillation converts the process of a technique’s execution and shortens it into a single call and response, producing an immediate but typically more crude result. While technique distillation can be used to produce a quicker result in place of the more refined technique, it is more often used in conjunction with a larger body of technique as a simplifying and standardizing tool.

---

## Basic Principles

### Compatibility

As technique distillation produces a _distilled_ form of the technique in question, the product of technique distillation can always be fed directly back into the technique from which it came. This guarantee of compatibility allows technique distillation to act as an adapter between two normally incongruent techniques, yielding novel results that cannot be achieved by other cataphatic techniques.

**A technique distillation creates an overlap between two subsets of signs.**

The innate overall compatibility of distilled techniques also lends well to serving as a foundational element of a technique framework. A technique distilled from set of cataphatic and apophatic techniques can be fed directly into a more generalized framework of apophatic techniques, allowing for the practical extension of a more specialized technique.

### High Efficiency, Low Output

As one would expect from the name, technique distillation extracts only a part of the overall technique, leaving the rest behind. This residue releases itself more readily than other higher-output techniques, and thus heavily mitigates the problem of exhaustion compared to less efficient cataphatic techniques. However, this efficiency necessitates a significantly diminished maximum and average output compared to other cataphatic techniques, making technique distillation a poor choice when considering raw magnitude of results.

## Applications

Technique distillation finds itself in two extremely common use cases: in complex technique frameworks, and among highly experienced practitioners who engage in frequent combat. In both cases, distillation serves a constituent role—rarely taking center stage, but operating as a key component in the background. Distillation typically plays three roles:

- **Cycling**: Many techniques store or produce undesirable or less-useful products when they are used often or constantly. By incorporating technique distillation into the process of production, less desired products can be converted back into usable material to fuel another technique in the process—potentially the original technique.

- **Coherence**: As mentioned before, technique distillation can help generalize a specialized technique or connect disparate techniques together. Many polychromatic technique frameworks use this fact to create techniques of antagonistic Colors. Practitioners who have attained the understanding of [[Irrealis Technique]] often consider distillation coherence a critical step in understanding the principle behind elemental simulacra.

- **Convergence**: Considered the most advanced use of technique distillation, convergence applies the distillation process to each aspect of a technique framework and funnels all products into a receiving technique, usually apophatic. The result is an encapsulating technique that is capable of simultaneously executing an entire sequence of techniques at once, producing a technique that is somehow more efficient despite being comprised of distilled techniques. Many practitioners achieve their first pinnacle technique through distillation convergence.

A large number of applications stem from the first two forms of technique distillation alone:

- Techniques that depend on the propagation of an elemental medium use distillation cycling to recycle unused products of the techniques back into the medium for reuse.

- Highly antagonistic Colors like Indigo and Green can be stabilized via an intermediary technique via distillation coherence. This fact is essential in the act of Deeping.

- Cyclical techniques that transition between states, such as game-like or rules-based techniques often use a combination of coherence and cycling to maintain the game or rule state after its activation, allowing for perpetual maintenance of the technique as long as the conditions are met.

- [[Manifestation Technique]]s that partially manifest a large number of unwieldy summons are often distilled and cycled into other summons or used directly.

However, convergence is what truly elevated technique distillation in the hands of an experienced practitioner:

- Straightforward technique frameworks benefit greatly from their pinnacle techniques being forged from convergence—the efficiency of activation combined with the typically already overkill output mitigates all weaknesses of distillation while granting the practitioner access to readily-available pinnacle techniques.

- Particularly well-lived practitioners with an ever-growing store of tools can refurbish older and less useful tools permanently via distillation convergence, holding the fused tools via an apophatic technique like a [[Domain Technique]] or even imbued into another receiving tool via [[Barrier Technique]].

- Those practitioners at the apex of combat technique often use distillation convergence for its incredibly fast activation in conjunction with [[Domain Technique]]. The result is a blazing-fast activation of technique combined with a typically nigh-inescapable deployment of a barrier—a near-guaranteed victory.

Note: There have been practitioners who have questioned this article’s level of praise for technique distillation, questioning if it is excessive. It is not.

## Mechanical Considerations

Technique distillation is a great way to shortcut complicated technique sequences or reduce the clutter of too many summons, both of which can become highly cumbersome for both DM and player if things get out of hand. It is also highly recommended to use distillation as a way to experiment with combining techniques or creating Color combinations to access polychromatic spell types!
