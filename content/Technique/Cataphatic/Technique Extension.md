Technique Extension is the expansion of a technique’s existing conditions and effects. Extension of a technique differs from the creation of an alternate technique with additional features as technique extension’s apophatic nature allows for more efficient extension of technique while adding less or no additional signs. Technique Extension features heavily in higher-level technique frameworks as a way to generalize a technique to all conceptual levels of a framework, and this maneuverability around levels of abstraction allows combat practitioners to use technique extension as a way to circumvent an opponent’s defenses.

---

## Basic Principles

### Stepwise Extension

When a practitioner uses technique extension to alter the conceptual level or broadness of their technique’s condition, this adjustment can only be done once at a time, in one direction at a discrete level. As a result, expansion of a technique’s target in real space cannot be done continuously using technique extension. Instead, one would have to create a discrete step onto which a practitioner may use extension. For this reason, many practitioners encapsulate technique extension within a [[Structural Technique]] that allows them to collapse a sign sequence used for technique extension into a higher order sacrificial rite.

### Cyclical Levels

The [[Technique]] necessitates that sufficient movement in any conceptual direction eventually return oneself to the original level. As technique extension can move through these levels of abstraction rather quickly compared to other methods, this cyclical nature of abstraction is to be kept in mind, both to prevent unwanted technique extensions and as a quick shortcut to a potentially harder-to-access level of abstraction.

## Applications

Extensions of technique operate on many levels, with the primary being **target extension**. By adjusting the abstraction of a technique’s innate target, the practitioner is capable of generalizing the target criteria of a technique for many applications beyond the initial scope of the technique. Below is a list of common uses of target extension:

- Generalizing a class of techniques to a larger scope.

- Selectively bypassing certain target restrictions, such as altered spaces, immovable obstacles, or any other type of unreachable target.

- Applying a technique en masse to a large group of targets with a single use of a technique.

- Targeting highly specific ontological structures with more powerful techniques.

More advanced forms of technique extension also exist, with the most exalted among them being the use of [[Irrealis Technique]]s to extend a property of the technique into non-real territories.

## Mechanical Considerations

Target extension is a key aspect of combat in the Hereafter, as a player’s defenses may become nigh-absolute in one level but not another. For example, the basic use of the phantasm to halt objects or cancel an opponent’s technique is powerful enough to stop almost any other technique that cannot simply overpower such a basic use, but altering the targeting criteria can allow the technique to entirely ignore these basic defenses.

  

Most forms of target extension exist as invocations, which require direct communication of intent via dialogue (free action) to use outside of a spell cast:

- "Spiral Staircase"—Declare with a theme of circumpolar motion. Ascend and gain 1 focus.
    
    - This invocation increases the level of abstraction of the technique’s target (ascends). For example, most techniques target an immediate contact area, but a technique ascended with this invocation would by default target the general area occupied by the contacted target.
    

- "Locust Swarm"—Declare with a theme of multitudinous motion. Expand and gain 1 focus.
    
    - This invocation expands the number of targets (expands). For example, expanding a typical technique that affects one target causes it to instead affect all targets of the same comparable category as the first affected target.
    

Mixing and matching these invocations is a core part of the Hereafter’s call-and-response cycle of attack and defense.
