Possibly one of the most straightforward cataphatic techniques, technique amplification is simply the direct intensification of one of the technique’s communicable signs or outputs. Its simplicity is unmatched by is ubiquity, as when does one not require _more_?

---

## Basic Principles

### Accumulating Exhaustion

Of all classes of cataphatic techniques, technique amplification dominate as the most efficient and least exhaustive techniques to use initially, but quickly outpace even creation techniques in terms of inefficiency as the amplification is maintained. This is due to the fact that technique amplification almost universally requires high amounts of maintained concentration combined with a constantly-evolving sign—a type of sign which becomes harder and harder to maintain, especially in moments of escalating tension.

**An amplification technique continually alters the sign itself.**

### **Diminishing Efficiency**

As a cataphatic technique, technique amplification cannot produce the same levels of unreal results that [[Irrealis Technique]]s are capable of outputting. instead, technique amplification steeply rises in inefficiency, upon which each additional point of linear returns incurs exponentially increasing cost. The sheer inefficiency of using technique amplification to produce novel or exotic results has been a major driver in the development of other cataphatic techniques.

Fortunately, technique amplification does not necessarily only apply to the output of the technique, as technique amplification can be used on any particular component in the sequence of signs that constitute a technique. This allows amplification to bypass some of its diminishing returns by intensifying various elements of more complicated techniques, potentially altering the outcome to produce novel results.

## Applications

Technique amplification is usually simply **output amplification**, or the direct amplification of a technique’s product. When used by a practitioner without proxies, output amplification draws focus away from the practitioner. Further amplification begins to directly drain focus instead, but offers even further amplification. The number of applications for output amplification are too numerous and uninteresting to list, and so we will not do so.

  

However, the rarer **priority amplification** mitigates the fundamental problems of technique amplification by operating on the priority of communicable signs as a whole, which are far more sensitive to changes than any process capable of being amplified by output amplification. This second type of technique amplification increases the priority of certain communicable signs and processes, causing them to dominate an interaction in which there is an order of occurrence.

  

The most common uses of priority amplifications are as follows:

- Preventing antagonistic phase interference between antagonist Colors from interfering with the effectiveness of a technique.

- Allowing slower and weaker techniques to temporarily bypass stronger techniques in a direct contest of effectiveness.

---

## Mechanical Considerations

Technique amplification may seem appealing due to its simple and effective nature, but the bonuses offered by technique amplification should be weighted carefully against simply attaining a more temporary bonus from elsewhere. That being said, technique amplification is perfectly suited to simply boosted the effect of movement-classed actions, with the effect amplified even further with the user of invocations and spells.

It is recommended that players do not proactively use technique amplification unless they are already at a tempo advantage.
