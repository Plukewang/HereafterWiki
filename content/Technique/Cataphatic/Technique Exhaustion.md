Technique exhaustion refers to the cataphatic and intentional exhaustion of a technique. Exhaustion occurs naturally with most techniques as a result of non-perfect efficiency producing waste products that remain in the environment (often in the practitioner), eventually reaching a saturation point in which no more technique can be used. By creatively inducing this effect in a controlled manner, the exhaustion effect can be controlled and used to the practitioner’s advantage.

---

## Basic Principles

### Output Vacuum

The direct consequence of exhaustion causes a saturation zone that prevents further output of the technique’s class in the saturation zone. This saturation is so strong as to prevent even normally produced communicable signs from taking effect in the zone. At the edge of this vacuum of output, real space becomes heavily saturated with the product of the exhausted technique, creating an expanding front of output that can be further controlled by the practitioner.

**Technique exhaustion displaces other cataphatic techniques with useless signs.**

### Inverted Efficiency

As the level of exhaustion directly relates to the effectiveness of technique exhaustion, less efficient cataphatic techniques produce a more effective technique exhaustion. This relationship is multiplicative to the effectiveness of the technique exhaustion itself, producing extremely powerful outputs compared even to direct [[Technique Amplification]].

---

## Applications

Technique exhaustion is typically avoided in more utilitarian use cases, being reserved primarily to more combat-oriented practitioners’ arsenals. That being said, intentional technique exhaustion, when applied with discretion, can become the most devastating tool in a practitioner’s arsenal due to the multiplicative effect from using an inefficient technique. Unfortunately, this means the number of demonstrable applications are few and far between, though there are some:

- When surroundings are not a concern, technique exhaustion is the most effective way to insulate an area from a specific Color or Element.

- Exhausting a technique aligned with a specific target can essentially eliminate the target from existence within the saturation zone.
