Domain techniques are a class of [[Technique]] that manipulate the [[Domain (Ontokinetics)|Domain]], the portion of a phantasm that occupies and exists in real space or its equivalents and applies a technique.
## Basic Principles

### Isomorphism of Domains

Domains techniques cannot change the fundamental topology of a domain without highly specific sacrificial vows and rites, and thus can only alter the structure of an underlying technique to one that preserves the structure of the technique.

A **domain technique is a structural technique of any order that limits each sign A to another sign B and vice versa.**

For concrete space, this isomorphism is the homotopy of the _n-_sphere, where _n_ is the number of dimensions of the underlying space. This implies that phantasms in real space cannot have true holes—as the topology of the _n_-sphere does not admit true holes. In order to produce alternative topologies for domains, one must construct a new category of domain.

### Finite Total Exortation

While the gradation of pressure can be manipulated through the regional differences in a phantasm’s domain, the total exortation throughout the domain does not change due to a isomorphism. Techniques that manipulate the domain to an extreme like the Compact Domain may appear initially to provide an infinite concentration at its boundaries, but said infinite concentration is a mere illusion. In situations where one practitioner’s exortative capacities undermines another’s, even infinite pressure cannot overcome this gap in capacity when countered correctly. This is why the supposedly infinite concentration of the piercing technique can only overcome that which a practitioner can output.

## Applications

As with other categories of apophatic techniques, domain techniques are a cornerstone of many ubiquitous techniques, especially in combat, where a clash of exortations typically determines the outcome between two opposing techniques. Sufficient pressure can allow a practitioner with lower total capacity to locally supersede a more powerful technique, as in the case of the compact domain and the piercing technique. The domain also exists as a vessel and template for the imbuing of technique itself, implying a vast and diverse array of potential combinations of technique and domain topology.

- Blink is a near-universal technique used for semi-instantaneous transport. Its most basic form is a shrinking of the domain to a degenerate one-dimensional line combined with a surge of motion which forcibly teleports the practitioner toward the point of focus.

- Compact domains use stereographic projection to convert the domain into a Euclidean plane, with one point of the domain representing infinity in all directions. This advanced technique essentially traps targets inside the domain due to the closed topology, and is significantly harder to escape from within. Creators of [[Barrier Technique]] tend to employ them alongside a compact domain for maximal isolation.

- Domain Constriction is an anti-eigenweapon technique that locally strengthens the domain in the wake of the piercing effects of an eigenweapon penetrating a phantasm. By constricting the domain after the initial wake of the eigenweapon’s attack, the practitioner may target and neutralize the weakest point of the attack.

- **Technique Inscription** imbues the currently active technique directly into the domain, allowing the technique to originate from any point within the domain with non-zero pressure. This method of technique activation differs subtly from simply applying a technique with a domain, as the effectiveness of the technique becomes constant throughout the domain, rather than being affected by the activation point’s pressure.

- **Imminent Domains** reifies a practitioner’s internal technical logic, manifesting the achievement of their desired technical result directly as their domain. Imminent domains are often considered the apex of combative techniques, as they both require a complete understanding of one’s technique and also automatically fulfill a practitioner’s success condition at the local level.

## Mechanical Considerations

Domains act as the determinant for a player’s effective range, as the current maximum range of one’s domain plus any existing barriers makes up the direct contact range of the player. As such, how one manipulates their domain is a critical point of skill expression for any player in the Hereafter, regardless of their playstyle.

  

Techniques ranges are calculated via reach from the source of the technique (either a barrier, a player, or an instance.) In most cases, increased range leads to reduced effectiveness, but this is not a rule. For instance:

- Blink—Moves up to 3 x RunSpeed instantly. Range: 3 x RunSpeed Effectiveness: 100%.

- Compact Domain—Target remains within reach; gain unlimited defense against trapped target except at point of infinity. Range: 1 x Reach, Effectiveness: 100%, decreasing toward infinity.

- Technique Inscription—Target technique maintains 100% effectiveness while within reach. Reach: 1x reach, Effectiveness: 100%.

- Domain Constriction—If target attacks or is attacking with eigenweapon, challenge PRC with effectiveness as bonus, if successful, apply [Immobilize]. Reach: 1x reach, Effectiveness: 50%.

- <Pierce> Disables user's defense. Ignore defense equal to the current action's highest affinity. Range = PRC Tier x Reach, Effectiveness: 100%.
