Technique inversion is an [[Technique]] that creates the complement of a technique. Direct complements of a technique annihilate with the semiotic process of the original technique, producing zero outcomes. This combined with the apophatic nature of technique inversion lends well to efficient and 100% effective countering of a technique compared to the negation of a technique via cataphatic means.

---

## Basic Principles

### Exposure

Inversion is a relatively simple concept, with the act itself being mostly intuitive. However, one important aspect of technique inversion is that the time complexity of the inversion process is directly proportional to the direct exposure intensity to the technique being inverted. The volume of exposure spans time, number of exposures, and magnitude of exposures. For example, it becomes significantly easier to invert a technique that interfaces with a barrier in every direction at a constant rate compared to a technique with short exposure time and frequency.

## Applications

Combat practitioners primarily use technique inversion to immediately counter an opponent’s offense and gain an advantage in tempo, or to mount a defense when the opponent presses the attack. Outside of combat, technique inversion exists primarily as a functional component in technique frameworks to quickly shut off the output of a continuous technique. This most common form of technique inversion is known as **[[Output Inversion]]**, and is the most commonly understood form of inversion.
  

A rarer form of technique inversion is **[[Structural Inversion]]**, or the full inversion of the semiotic processes of a technique. Its name, as one might expect, implies its relation to apophatic techniques, particularly to [[Structural Technique]]s. Due to the [[Technique]], structural inversion of a technique does not produce a technique that either outputs the complement or the input of the original technique. Where output inversion produces something akin to the negative of the technique, structural inversion produces the technique’s reciprocal, producing an identity element on the [[Algebraic Technique]]. This form of inversion in practice generates wildly different outputs based on the inverted technique:

- Inversion of a technique equipped with a higher-order structure created by a sacrificial vow produces a technique that when acting on the original technique produces the same technique without its higher-order structure.

- Inverted sacrificial rites produces a new technique that when used on the product of the sacrificial rite produces an output which satisfies all the minimum conditions of the sacrificial rites’ typical outputs.

In general, structural inversion can be said to produce a technique that has some reductive action on the inverted technique, such that the consecutive use of both the normal and inverted technique produces an output which does not change when acted upon by any other technique of the same category. ## Mechanical Considerations

Technique inversion for output inversion is a very simple form of counter-spell that takes one skill icon to execute, requiring the user to become exposed to the technique initially before it can be used. This exposure time depends on the stats of the player and the type of technique they are attempting to invert

  

- Cataphatic techniques without any support: 10 PRC.

- Cataphatic techniques imbued in a barrier: Max difficulty PRC or Affinity* check. Rolls higher than the requisite damage the barrier.

- Cataphatic techniques embedded in a domain: Challenge PRC or Affinity.

- Apophatic Techniques: Challenge PRC or Affinity.

*Check relevant affinity. Phase interference bonuses and penalties apply.

  

Structural inversion is far more complicated, and will be tackled on a case-by-case basis.
