Algebraic Techniques are a kind of [[Structural Technique | structural technique]] revolving around the abstract representation of a algebraic structures: a non-empty collection of objects with a set of operations and axioms enforced upon them. Of all apophatic techniques, algebraic techniques reign as the most obtuse and abstract, being a technique used either as a foundation or a supplement to more commonly-used techniques. Most practitioners will not interact with algebraic techniques, as they are primarily a feature designed and operated by high-level practitioners in charge of maintaining technique frameworks, but it will be included here for one’s own edification.

---

## Basic Principles

Proper understanding of algebraic techniques requires a solid logical foundation from which one may understand the theory of sets and categories. As such, the number of fundamental principles is too many to cover in this short description. Below is a non-exhaustive list of fundamental aspects of algebraic techniques:

- The subset of signs has a unary operation that defines relations between each sign.

- The subset of signs can take two signs and produce a product sign.

- The operations and the subset of signs must have a defined relationship that can be expressed axiomatically.

One may observe these requirements and realize many existing forms of mathematics and logic are algebraic techniques. That is in fact correct. One may also note that many natural processes follow these requirements. That is in fact correct. So long as a technique defines a subset of communicable signs and operations that follow at least some axioms, it is an algebraic technique.

**This is to say, an algebraic technique is a technique that underlies an algebra.**

---

## Applications

Beside defining highly-selective [[Structural Technique]] and creating complex ritual objects, algebraic techniques do in fact possess use for practitioners outside of those contexts:

- All forms of mathematics over an algebra are algebraic techniques by definition. The definition of a mathematical structure is an algebraic technique as long as the structure has at least one operation on a nonempty set of symbols.

- **Gauge Transformation** are a class of algebraic techniques that manipulate apophatic techniques, preserving the structure of the technique while altering any other variable within the scope of the transformed technique. This class of algebraic technique is typically used to alter the conditions of a barrier or domain to better encapsulate its target or fulfill the conditions of a technique’s activation.
