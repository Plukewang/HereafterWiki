Barrier techniques are a sub-category of [[Structural Technique | structural techniques ]]that are focused on the direct interface between the domain of the phantasm and reality, also know as a _barrier_. Barrier techniques form the foundation of most technique-using utilities, and are a fundamental component of any practitioner's offensive and defensive arsenal. The apophatic nature of barriers manifest in their strongly demarcating nature, changing the underlying nature of information that interfaces with the barrier.

Barriers are a prominent field of study that originate from various disciplines that revolve around the [[Technique]]. The strong assertion on reality that barriers offer promised a path to omnipotence whose ubiquitous access allowed countless practitioners to achieve the almighty before altering the nature of reality to render omnipotence unachievable via technique alone. Despite this, barrier technique practitioners continue to refine their barriers out of either sheer pursuit of the craft or the acceptance that nigh-omnipotence is good enough for them.

## Basic Principles

The foundations of all barrier techniques is the interface between the self-as-will and reality. Of all technique categories beside the apex of [[Domain Technique|domain techniques]], barrier techniques possess the strongest [[|priority]] on their interface space. As such, barrier techniques distinguish themselves from their sibling techniques by the elaborate and rules-changing nature of the barriers themselves. The strength of the barrier's interface also reinforces the priority and effectiveness of techniques used within the barrier, and thus they are often used in conjunction with cataphatic techniques to boost the potency and security of the overall technique.

### Barrier Definition

This priority originates from the fundamental principle of barriers: that they are formed from the limitation of perceptional resources to a set breadth and depth in reality based on some definite boundary condition. Following the subtractive nature of technique, it becomes ideal to either utilize an already-existed boundary, such as the interior of a building, or a simple locus in space like a radius to define the boundary of a barrier, as the strength of a barrier is directly proportional to the definition of the barrier's boundary conditions.

**Barrier techniques are first-order structural techniques that limit a larger subset to either within or without.**

Three major barrier types originate from this principle:

**[[Simple Barriers]]** utilize internal metrics such as intuitions of space to define the barrier. Users of simple barriers typically adjust their barrier's size and definition via active signs like gestures or commands with the perceptual center of attention being used as the spatial locus. This typically result in space-filling or spherical barriers whose primary draw is their flexibility and mobility. However, this makes simple barriers the easiest of the three main types to break. Despite this frailty, the flexibility of simple barriers make them a common tool in many practitioners' combat arsenal.

**[[Transient Barriers]]** define their boundaries via a real space, making them significantly more difficult to destroy than simple barriers. The strength and definition of transient barriers depend almost entirely on the strength and definition of the actual boundary, and thus transient barriers are most often used when the space is already well-defined, often as a way to imbue further technique into an object.

**[[Fixed Barriers]]** require neither internal nor real metrics, allowing for full freedom in defining the boundary of the barrier. Practitioners instead utilize complex sequences of signs and auxiliary techniques to create a custom boundary with a definition proportional to the refinement of the practitioner's technique. The independence from real and internal metrics renders the prospect of raising a fixed barrier nigh-impossible for most practitioners, but the independence and reliance on refinement of fixed barriers make them ideal for exceptional barrier practitioners.

### Boundary Continuity

Beyond the definition of a barrier's boundary, the boundary conditions of a barrier also concern the continuity or lack thereof between the outside and inside of the barrier. The selectivity of a barrier depends entirely on the continuity conditions of a barrier, with increasing discontinuity yielding decreasing permittivity. One may consider discontinuous barriers as emphasizing inclusion and exclusion, with permissive barriers focusing on detection and selection.

**The relation → defined on a barrier has a continuity defined by the subset of signs that have the within/without relation.**

In general, the continuity conditions of a barrier can be categorized as follows:

[[|Cosms]] are entirely causally discontinuous barriers that entirely exclude all events and communicable signs from crossing the boundary of the barrier. They are also known by the misnomer of the "edge of the world". Unlike some practitioners' misguided guesses, the boundary conditions of a cosm are not in fact determined by the divinity(s) that occupy the cosm. The conditions that lead to the natural formation of cosms as barrier phenomena are unknown.

[[|Shells]] are exclusionary barriers that practitioners make entirely opaque from the outside, preventing communicable signs and events from crossing the boundary of the barrier. However, unlike cosms, shells are causally continuous with the outside of the barrier, rendering them one step removed from a full cosm. Despite shells' opacity, they are not considered cromulent in considerations of defensive techniques. Shells distinguish themselves in use in situations where real space discontinuities between inside and outside serve great purpose.

[[|Veils]] selectively exclude communicable signs between outside and inside, but are permeable to events crossing the boundary of the barrier. Veil barriers are often employed as transient barriers to conceal certain practitioners' operations and to maintain secrecy or a sense of normalcy. Unlike shells, veils are typically continuous in real space.

[[|Filters]] selectively include and exclude signs and events continuously between inside and outside, without allowing included or excluded elements from crossing the boundary. The maximization of selectivity indicates filter barriers as optimal for defensive measures, but the far more common use of filters is the implementation of various technique frameworks through the realization of logical sorting.

[[|Fields]] inclusively detect and select events and signs that enter the barrier. It is often the misguided guess of an amateur practitioner that [[Domain (Ontokinetics)|Domains]] are fields, but this is false. The open nature of fields grants them flexibility, and this flexibility is most often exploited through the imbuing of further techniques into the barrier.

[[|The Interstice]] exists as a logical result of the existence of barriers and cosms, acting as a universal connector that serves as a guaranteed point of continuity between all barriers, including cosms. Ontological scholars have long debated whether the Interstice's existence came about as a logical extension of the same phenomenon that instantiate cosms, or whether the two exist as a dual of the [[|Inside-Outside Relationship]] at a fundamental level. Said debate is a battlefield rife with politics and personal bias, and will not be discussed.

It is important to note that these are general categories of description, and thus one may find unique barriers with continuity conditions that do not fit these criteria.

## Applications

Barrier techniques are some of the most used apophatic techniques and are the most often comprehended among the general populace, even among those who seldom practice technique of any kind. Ubiquitous applications of technique such as "enchantments", "seals", and "wards" are all founded on a basis of barrier technique. Below is a non-exhaustive list of common barrier technique applications:

- Technique imbuement, also known as "enchantment"; the imbuing of a latent technique into an object or space via a transient field.

- [[Instance Partition]], the separation of a portion of a practitioner's domain into a disconnected partition in real space with the use of a simple barrier.

- [[Deeping]], the ritualistic process by which one constructs and enters an [[Ontological Space]] bounded by a fixed shell.

### Mechanical Considerations

Barrier techniques are some of the most powerful applications of technique that can exist. They can be used to defensively hold against a technique or offensively overcome another technique by virtue of the strength of barriers. However, unlike other techniques, barriers can be directly destroyed, rendering them objects of extremely high strategic value.

  

Barrier HP is typically rendered as a shield—that is to say that they benefit directly from affinities and not much else.
