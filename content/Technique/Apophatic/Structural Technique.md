Structural techniques are a foundational category of [[Technique]] that establish rules and guidelines that other techniques follow. The use of structural techniques gives rise to new reflections of a singular technique, and is an important step in overcoming plateaus of understanding for any practitioner. Beyond practitioners, even the divine practice structural techniques, particularly among each other.

## Basic Principles

### Symmetry Breaking

The one and only principle of structural techniques is that they disrupt an already-existing symmetry, with the number of new products created by the structural technique denoting the **order** of the structure. A technique that takes one initial symmetry and renders it into two complementary techniques is a first-order structure, while a second-order structure divides into three complementary techniques.

**Structural techniques impose limitations to what signs any individual sign can produce. In other words, they define a relation for every sign in a subset.**

This denotation of order is important to the final products of a structural technique, as higher order structures possess greater levels of complexity and eventually produce more insignificant noise than communicable signs.

## Applications

The applications of structural techniques elucidates their categorization as symmetry-breaking techniques:

- **[[Sacrificial Vow|Sacrificial Vows]]** splits local causality into first-order branches via the conditions of the vow, creating complementary rewards and punishments based on the upkeep or violation of the vow’s conditions.
    
    Practitioners often differentiate a more general technique into sub-techniques via sacrificial vows, enhancing a specific aspect of a technique while reducing the power of less-valued aspects.
    
    Sacrificial vows play a foundational role in the structure of reality, as the nature of causality and logic shared by all realities are maintained via permanent* sacrificial vows made between and under various divinities. Practitioners may also undergo sacrificial vows with greater powers in order to acquire new traits and techniques:
    
    1. A God, who provides a **blessing** that exists as a special trait. Blessings grant a passive benefit in exchange for a punishment in violation of the vow’s conditions. For example, a blessing from the One-Eyed Hawk provides a once-per-session use to automatically succeed a theoretically achievable agility check, but will cause all subsequent agility checks to fail if the blessed individual does harm to a bird.
    
    1. A chthonic entity, which provides a **legacy** that can be activated at any time with some form of drawback. In general, the broken symmetry of a legacy is the disparity between the legacy’s benefits and its costs.
        
        For example, Morgan Avici’s eponymous legacy “Scarlet Thorn” allows for the creation of a weapon that is guaranteed to kill its target regardless of toughness or protection, but its continued upkeep requires a constant stream of death that only allows the weapon to manifest for longer than a moment if thousands of people die in seconds.
        
    
    1. A primordial source, which provides a **divergence** which directly restricts or prevents certain actions or skills while providing an innate benefit. Unlike blessings, divergences cannot be withdrawn or violated in the same way, and thus do not have punishments.
        
        For example, Zatos’ natural-born abilities are product of a divergence that granted him extreme perceptive capabilities and near-precognitive sensitivity to patterns and the environment in exchange for the extreme psychological strain on a human mind. This was furthered compounded by a separate divergence inherited as a recessive trait which drastically increased physical strength and durability in exchange for the inability to perceive Color.
        
        These divergences combined with Zatos’ own neural divergences (not of the sacrificial vow type) would have already produced a prodigious warrior with near-endless potential, but the acquisition of a third unknown divergence appears to have somehow lifted the restrictions of his second divergence, allowing him to also wield Color. Terrifying.
        
    
    1. Another individual of equal Level, which creates a **contract** whose conditions are forged through a direct communication of agreement. The nature of sacrificial vows as contracts is relatively poorly understood, as most supposed contracts are usually a form of coercion or borrowed power. However, it is known that the God’s Eye gained their first All-Seeing Eye as part of a contract with the yet-to-be One-Eyed-Hawk before its ascension to godhood.
    

- **[[Sacrificial Rite|Sacrificial Rites]]** create higher-order structures via the codification of specific signs to produce specific conditions and not others, essentially creating a technique with multiple complementary alterations that cannot be achieved without a structural technique.
    
    Practitioners often utilize sacrificial rites to produce heritable or transferable techniques. The reproducibility and sequential nature of sacrificial rites also allows for the formation of locally commutative signs within a specific technique framework. One such framework is the Rite of Inmost Light, a set of broadly-applicable sacrificial rites with combat purposes codified before the current epoch of reality.
    
- **[[Barrier Technique|Barriers]]** are higher-order structures at the edge of a phantasm, encoding specific conditions upon the edge which can act on the spaces it separates.

### Mechanical Considerations

Sacrificial vows and rites are an avenue toward greater power, either externally through the power of others or internally through the differentiation of already-existing techniques. A player will usually encounter them when making deals with powerful entities or when making them with themselves.

Examples:

- Tomoe Shinazuhara’s close-quarters attacks are a highly-refined form of Overlapping Resonance combined with Perspective Amplification, both technique amplifications, but their powers are further elevated through a sacrificial vow.

Overlapping Resonance: <clean hit>, <guard crush>, <follow through>, increase final damage by PRC tier.

Perspective Amplification: <Twilight>, automatically trigger [Lucent]’s purge effect on hit with Verity Tier - 2 x increased effectiveness.

Combine normally, these techniques produce the combination of results without <guard crush>, but with Tomoe’s sacrificial vow:

Assault: <Twilight> <clean hit>, <guard crush>, <follow through>, increase final damage by PRC tier * Verity Tier -2 x effectiveness; automatically trigger [Lucent]’s purge effect on hit. Damage reduced to 1 for all but the main target hit.
 
Demolish: <Twilight> <clean hit>, <guard crush>, <follow through>, <inertia>, increase final damage by PRC tier * Verity Tier x effectiveness; automatically trigger [Lucent]’s purge effect on hit. Damage reduced to 1 for anything not an inanimate object.

    Sacrificial vows are also used in barrier techniques to ensure the stability of a barrier. For example, the Star technique uses a sacrificial vow ensuring that the conditions of the barrier will not change to strengthen the stability of the instance.

Sacrificial Rites are even more complex, but only in their study. Sacrificial Rites are simply how actual skills and techniques are created for players and how other players may access them.
