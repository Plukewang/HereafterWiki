Irrealis techniques are a diverse category of [[Technique]]that operate on a lack of communicable signs, hence the use of the term “irrealis.” Unlike structural techniques, which create their differentiated outcomes via the breaking of logical symmetries, irrealis techniques do not conserve reality, instead negating the negative to create a positive outcome. Due to the [[Technique]], irrealis techniques are generally deemed some of the most difficult and complex techniques to exist.

## Basic Principles

### Counterfactual Information

Irrealis techniques do not produce communicable signs directly, instead tracing the implicit absence of the desired result. It is this nature that makes many advanced practitioners consider irrealis techniques to be the epitome of apophatic technique. It might thus be better to say that irrealis techniques can be considered a form of ontological inception that brings something into existence via proof by contradiction. For practitioners unable to envision the absence of a thing as well as the existence of a thing, such a proof is far more difficult to execute as technique than theory.

**Irrealis techniques impose limits on other limits. In other words, irrealis un-defines the assignment of signs.**

### Multiple Negation

The contradictory process of irrealis technique implies at minimum a two-step process of negation and subsequent abnegation of the negation. A common theoretical example used to teach the basis of irrealis technique is the multiplication of two negative integers to make one positive integer, essentially mapping two objects in one plane of existence onto another plane entirely. While this at first seems like an adequate explanation, true irrealis technique generates results far more contradictory than a change of numerical domain.

**Irrealis techniques implicitly define subsets where structural techniques explicitly define them**_**.**_

Other scholars attempt to dumb down the concept of irrealis techniques by explaining the abnegation of negation as simply a shift of perspective, that the production of paradoxical results is that alone: a paradox—an inconsistency in perspective that can be resolved via the reframing of the problem. This is in many ways even more inferior than the simple negative-negative-positive explanation, as it completely obfuscates if not outright denies the contradiction essential to irrealis techniques.

## Applications

It is important to note that irrealis techniques are perhaps the most often misattributed form of technique, with many practitioners proclaiming their mastery over the art when they have failed to even begin to understand the basis of the technique itself. As such, here is a scholarly reminder:

- Reversal or denial of a technique is not irrealis technique.

- Transposition of a technique into another framework is not irrealis technique.

- Producing a novel result from haphazardly slapping together techniques is not irrealis technique.

  

With that addressed, it becomes important to note that the extremely unique nature of irrealis techniques makes a compilation of their most common applications essentially an oxymoronic endeavor. As such, here is simply a (non-exhaustive) list of known applications that are not unique to one individual:

- **Reverse Causality** is by far the most common type of irrealis technique and also the most often misattributed. Reverse causality differs fundamentally from, for example, Blue causal reversal as it is not in fact the reversal of a directed process, but rather an entirely new process that violates all known symmetries of normal causality. This technique is the only known way for practitioners to directly undo true death and remove the saturation sickness associated with Technique Exhaustion.

- **Elemental Simulacra** are another badly-named technique that do not simulate an Element with another, but rather creates an entirely new Element via the paired and contradictory understanding between two diametrically opposed Colors. Those who have witnessed the azure bestowal flames will know its awesome power.
